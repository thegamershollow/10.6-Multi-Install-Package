Verify the startup volume

You can use OnyX to verify the structure of system files on the startup volume and to provide the option of running this verification upon opening OnyX. Before running this verification, OnyX will quit all open applications. (Some tasks can influence the results of this check.)

Important: During the verification process, the Finder may become slow or unresponsive. The wait cursor (which looks like a colored pinwheel) may show up, or your computer may appear to be unresponsive. This is normal, so wait for the verification to finish!

Warning: This verification option is only possible if the hard disk is journaled.

Open OnyX, and type your administrator password.

Click the Verify icon, then select the Structure of volume tab.

Click the Check button to immediately verify the startup volume.

By default, OnyX asks whether you want to check the structure of the startup volume at OnyX launch. You can choose to automatically check it or to do nothing by selecting the corresponding option in the Advanced tab of the OnyX Preferences pane.
